Summary & Strategic Communications

Indiana is experiencing an acute energy affordability crisis, with residential electric bills rising an average of 17.5% in a single year (2024–2025) — the steepest year-over-year increase in two decades — driven by a wave of IURC-approved rate hikes at Duke Energy Indiana, NIPSCO, AES Indiana, and CenterPoint Energy. The crisis has prompted an unprecedented political response: Governor Mike Braun replaced IURC commissioners, appointed a new consumer-focused Utility Consumer Counselor, signed landmark legislation (HEA 1002) introducing performance-based ratemaking and low-income protections, and the IURC launched a statewide Investigative Inquiry on Energy Affordability in early 2026. Simultaneously, a rapidly expanding AI data center buildout across Indiana — particularly tied to NIPSCO's GenCo/Amazon deal — is raising new concerns from consumer advocates that future power demand will drive further rate increases for residential customers.

Indiana legislators and Governor's office
Performance-based ratemaking under HEA 1002 is a critical first step, but without a binding affordability standard — such as capping residential bill increases — and stronger IURC enforcement authority, Hoosier families remain exposed to the next round of utility rate requests; the data center growth wave makes action more urgent, not less.
IURC commissioners and OUCC
The IURC's investigative inquiry has rebuilt public trust — now regulators must translate that momentum into durable, formal affordability standards, mandatory low-income assistance program quality benchmarks, and rigorous oversight of data center cost allocation to ensure residential ratepayers do not subsidize industrial growth.
General Indiana public and media
Indiana's affordability crisis is not inevitable: it is the result of years of state policy choices that guaranteed utility profits regardless of performance. The same policy process that created this crisis can fix it — through transparent rate proceedings, consumer participation, and demanding utilities earn their returns by delivering reliable, affordable service.

WFYI Public Media · 2026-04-21
Gov. Braun signs HEA 1002 to lower Indiana energy costs

Governor Mike Braun signed House Enrolled Act 1002, landmark legislation that overhauls Indiana's utility regulation by introducing performance-based ratemaking and requiring utilities to meet measurable benchmarks for affordability and reliability. The law also requires utilities to offer low-income customer assistance programs by July 1, 2026, and automatically enrolls eligible low-income households in levelized billing plans. Unlike the previous system that effectively guaranteed utilities a return on equity, HEA 1002 ties returns to performance.
