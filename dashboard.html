Summary & Strategic Communications

Indiana is experiencing a significant energy affordability crisis, with all five major investor-owned utilities having sought or received rate increases in the past 12–18 months, drawing unprecedented public backlash and regulatory scrutiny. In February 2026 the IURC launched a formal investigative inquiry into the 'big five' utilities, followed by statewide listening sessions, while Governor Braun signed House Enrolled Act 1002 into law on February 26, 2026, introducing performance-based ratemaking, mandatory low-income assistance programs, and a new summer disconnection moratorium for low-income customers. Federal threats to eliminate LIHEAP funding compound the pressure on vulnerable Hoosier households already struggling with bills that have grown faster than incomes.

Gov. Braun signs House Bill 1002 to tackle Indiana's energy affordability crisis

Governor Braun signed House Enrolled Act 1002, a major bipartisan utility reform law, on February 26, 2026. The law requires multi-year rate plans with performance incentive mechanisms tied to affordability and reliability, mandates low-income customer assistance programs, creates a summer disconnection moratorium for low-income households, and requires quarterly data reporting to the OUCC. The new levelized billing and assistance program requirements take effect July 1, 2026.
