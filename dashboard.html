Messaging Recommendations Silverman & Smith

Indiana Legislators and IURC Commissioners
Rising utility rates are forcing Indiana families to choose between heat and food — the state must strengthen low-income protections and scrutinize rate case requests that shift costs onto those least able to pay.
Framing the issue around concrete household tradeoffs appeals to both fiscal responsibility and constituent welfare, making it harder for policymakers to dismiss consumer concerns as special-interest advocacy.
Low-income ratepayers and community organizations
You have a right to affordable energy and protection from unfair disconnection — free legal and financial assistance resources exist, and your voice in utility proceedings matters.
Empowerment-based messaging grounded in rights language reduces fatalism and increases engagement with available assistance programs and public comment opportunities.
General Indiana public and media
Indiana utility companies are requesting some of the largest rate increases in decades while low-income households already spend a disproportionate share of their income on energy — accountability and transparency in the rate-setting process protects everyone.
Connecting individual household impact to broader systemic accountability broadens the coalition beyond low-income advocates and frames utility oversight as a universal public interest issue.
Nonprofit and community action agencies
Coordinated outreach and IURC testimony from community organizations amplifies the consumer record in rate cases and can directly influence commission decisions on affordability conditions and assistance programs.
Demonstrating that organized participation produces tangible outcomes incentivizes institutional engagement and builds durable advocacy capacity across the state.

Indiana Disconnection Moratorium Debates Return as Winter Ends

Consumer advocates are renewing calls for Indiana to adopt stronger statutory protections against utility disconnection for vulnerable customers, particularly as winter moratorium periods expire. Unlike some neighboring states, Indiana does not have a blanket winter shutoff prohibition for all residential customers, relying instead on utility tariff provisions. The IURC held a stakeholder workshop in early 2024 to examine existing disconnection rules.

LIHEAP Funding Falls Short as Indiana Energy Bills Rise

Federal LIHEAP assistance for Indiana households has not kept pace with rising utility costs, leaving community action agencies scrambling to stretch limited dollars further. Indiana's community action network reports turning away eligible families due to funding exhaustion earlier in the heating season than in prior years. Advocates are calling on the state legislature to appropriate additional emergency energy assistance funds.

Energy Burden Study Finds Indiana Low-Income Households Among Most Strained in Midwest

A new study from a regional energy policy center found that low-income Indiana households spend on average more than 10% of household income on energy costs, placing Indiana among the more burdened states in the Midwest. The research points to older housing stock, reliance on natural gas for heating, and a series of recent rate increases as key drivers. The authors recommend expanded weatherization funding and stronger utility low-income program requirements.
